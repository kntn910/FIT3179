Volcanoes on Earth

Where are volcanoes located?

Volcanoes are natural mountains that are formed by the impact of tectonic plate collision
and so they are often found along these plate boundaries.

The map shows the distribution of volcanoes across the Earth and its tectonic plates. As observed,
countries like the United States, Japan and Russia have the most number of volcanoes. In fact,
the volcanoes' placements follow the edges of the Pacific, Philippine Sea and Sunda tectonic plates.
We call this traced line the Ring of Fire.

Types of Volcanoes

Stratovolcanoes are the most commonly occurring volcanoes on Earth, making up a total of 927.
They is the top-ranking volcano type across the top 10 countries with the most number of volcanoes.

Elevation Distribution

Volcanoes tend to be up to 2000m above sea level whereas some can reach 6000m and higher.
Meanwhile, elevations below sea level pertain to underwater volcanoes with the lowest being
6000m below sea.

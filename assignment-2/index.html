Volcanoes on Earth

Volcanoes are natural mountains that are formed by the impact
of tectonic plate collisions in the Earth's crust. Earth itself
is home to 2662 volcanoes. Read on for a summary about our volcanoes.

Where are volcanoes found?

The map shows the distribution of volcanoes across the Earth and its tectonic plates.
The United States, Japan and Russia have the most number of volcanoes and in fact,
we observe they form a brilliant line along the border of the land famously known
known as the Ring of Fire.

This belt of volcanoes that encircle the Pacific Ocean or Pacific tectonic plate is a particular
hot spot as volcanoes tend to form along the boundary between tectonic plates.

What volcanoes exist on Earth?

Volcanoes are diverse; they range from the majestic, to colossal shield
volcanoes, to volcanic fields that can cover hundreds of square kilometres.
Stratovolcanoes are the most commonly occurring volcanoes on Earth
followed by shield volcanoes.

Do volcanoes exist in the ocean?

The greatest number of Earth's volcanoes occur on the ocean floor
along spreading ridges. Elevations below sea level pertain to
underwater volcanoes with the lowest being 6000m below sea.
On the other hand, land volcanoes tend to be up to 3000m high
with others reaching heights of 7000m.
